Before the redesign, the Complutense University of Madrid Information Service had a fragmented visual identity, with inconsistent styles and a hard-to-read logo. The brand guidelines were unclear, and the website suffered from poor navigation, visual clutter, and inconsistent design. Social media content lacked coherence and adaptation to each platform, while brand materials showed technical errors and inconsistency. Limited budget and non-specialized staff made it urgent to develop a more sustainable, accessible, and professional communication system, which I did as part of my internship in this office
The logo uses a puzzle-piece metaphor to represent the connection of ideas. The letters “C” and “I” interact playfully, forming either two characters or a single smiling face through Gestalt closure. The filled and outlined forms reflect the diversity of the audience.

modified characters
The Gulax typeface was selected for the logo, but specific characters were modified using the open-source software FontForge to better align the type with the academic and informational identity of CompluInforma.

Employee credential design and informative guide brochure template
This project relies on the formation of non specialized staff to create consistent and clean graphic design results through the open-source platform PenPot, in which employees can find templates of different forms of content created by me, which comply to the brand’s image and guidelines.
This is complemented by the Graphic Design Handbook for office staff, which includes detailed tutorials on content creation applying the brand’s image through PenPot and approachable informative content on graphic design principles for digital content.

Information Service Official Web Page
The official web page for the Information Service was redesigned as well and implemented officially in January 2025, improving previous user experience and interface. Every graphic in the web was made as a template in PenPot so any member of the team can easily edit it.

This Bachelor’s Degree Thesis was graded Honours, the maximum and extraordinary grade that can be obtained in a subject at university in Spain, and was awarded Second Prize for Best Inclusive Bachelor’s Degree Thesis by Cátedra Once Tiflotecnología for its improvements in the area of accessibility in the design academic field.

These results highlight the need to continue investing in accessible and sustainable tools, collaborative working methods, and an understanding of design that goes beyond aesthetics, as a key strategy for innovation and adaptation within a university environment shaped by economic uncertainty.
